Stanley Kubrick was an American filmmaker best known for directing Dr. Strangelove, 
Clockwork Orange, 2001: A Space Odyssey, The Shining and Full Metal Jacket.

Born in New York City on July 26, 1928, Stanley Kubrick worked as a photographer for 
Look magazine before exploring filmmaking in the 1950s. He went on to direct a number 
of acclaimed films, inclduing Spartacus (1960), Lolita (1962), Dr. Strangelove (1964), 
Clockwork Orange (1971), 2001: A Space Odyssey (1968), The Shining (1980), Full Metal 
Jacket (1987) and Eyes Wide Shut (1999). Kubrick died in England on March 7, 1999.
